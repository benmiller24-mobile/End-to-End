## Counter-Depth Design Implications for Eclipse Kitchens

**Standard counter depth = 25"–25.5"** (24" cabinet + 1"–1.5" countertop overhang).

Counter-depth refrigerators are **24" deep** (body only, excluding doors and handles), which means:

- The **door + handle** will protrude **1"–4"** beyond the counter face depending on the model.
- Built-in/integrated models (Sub-Zero, Miele integrated, Fisher & Paykel, Thermador panel-ready) protrude the **least** — typically 1"–2" including the panel door.
- Freestanding counter-depth (KitchenAid, some Miele) protrude **3"–5"** with handles — still far less than a standard 30"+ deep fridge.
- **AI design rule:** When calculating fridge clearance in a plan, use **27" total depth** as a safe planning dimension for counter-depth units (body + door + handle). Use **25"** for fully integrated panel-ready units.

| Miele | ~27"–28" | **~3"–4"** |
| Wolf Pro | ~28.25"–29.5" | **~4"–5.5"** |

**AI design rule:** Pro-style ranges are always deeper than base cabinets. Expect **3"–5" of protrusion** past the cabinet face. Thermador Pro Harmony is the exception — nearly flush with cabinets.

## 5. VENTILATION / RANGE HOODS

All five brands offer range hoods. Key sizing rules:

| Range Width | Minimum Hood Width | Recommended Hood Width |
|---|---|---|
| 30" | 30" | 36" |
| 36" | 36" | 42" |
| 48" | 48" | 54" |
| 60" | 60" | 66" |

**Rule:** Hood should be **at least as wide as the cooking surface**, ideally **3"–6" wider per side**.

### Mounting Heights (all brands)
- Gas cooktop: **30"–36"** from cooktop to hood bottom
- Electric/Induction cooktop: **24"–30"** from cooktop to hood bottom
- High-BTU pro ranges (Wolf, Thermador Pro Grand): use **36"** minimum

### CFM Requirements
- Standard cooking: **300–600 CFM**
- Pro-style gas ranges (36"): **600–900 CFM**
- Pro-style gas ranges (48"–60"): **900–1,200 CFM**
- **Rule of thumb for gas:** 100 CFM per 10,000 BTU of total burner output
- **Rule of thumb for wall-mounted:** 100 CFM per linear foot of cooktop width
- **Rule of thumb for island-mounted:** 150 CFM per linear foot (needs more power due to lack of back wall)

## 6. WALL OVENS

Standard widths across all five brands: **24"**, **27"**, **30"**. All are designed for built-in cabinet installation.

| Width | Typical Cutout W | Typical Cutout H (single) | Typical Cutout H (double) | Depth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 24" | 22¾"–23¾" | ~23"–24" | ~44"–46" | 22"–24" |
| 27" | 25¾"–26¾" | ~25"–26" | ~50"–51" | 22"–24" |
| 30" | 28¾"–29¾" | ~27"–28" | ~50"–53" | 22"–24" |

**AI design rule:** A wall oven in a tall cabinet requires the oven to be installed so the **opened door is at approximately counter height** (36") — this means the oven cavity center is at roughly **42"–48"** from the floor (ergonomic zone). Never install a wall oven with the bottom of the cavity above 54" (unsafe to reach in and lift heavy dishes).

## 7. SUMMARY: ECLIPSE KITCHEN DESIGN RULES DERIVED FROM THESE SPECS

1. **Counter-depth is the default.** All refrigerators should be 24" deep (body). Plan for 25"–27" total depth including doors/handles.

2. **Built-in fridges are 84" tall.** Plan a tall enclosure or cabinet surround. KitchenAid freestanding counter-depth units are only ~70" tall — they need a cabinet or soffit above to look built-in.

3. **Pro ranges protrude.** Expect 3"–5" past the cabinet face (except Thermador Pro Harmony at ~0.5"). Don't try to make the counter flush with the range front — let the range protrude and ensure landing counter is still usable.

4. **Thermador cooktops are 37" actual width.** Don't assume 36" — verify cutout sizing against the actual model selected.

5. **Wolf is the deepest range brand** (~28"–29.5"). If using Wolf, plan for the countertop to terminate at the range opening rather than trying to wrap behind it.

6. **Island cooktops need 27"+ depth minimum** in the counter section where they're installed (21" cooktop + clearance).

7. **DishDrawers unlock island flexibility.** Fisher & Paykel single DishDrawers at ~17" tall can fit under low island counters where a full-height dishwasher can't.

8. **Hood width = cooking surface width + 6"** (3" per side) for best capture. An undersized hood is a top designer complaint.

9. **Hood CFM for pro gas = 100 CFM per 10,000 BTU.** A Wolf 36" with 6 burners at ~15,000 BTU each = 90,000 BTU → need ~900 CFM.

10. **Wall oven sweet spot = oven center at 42"–48" from floor.** Never above 54".

11. **All brands offer panel-ready options** for integrated fridge and dishwasher. Eclipse kitchens should default to panel-ready for a seamless look.

12. **18" dishwashers (Miele) are useful** as secondary dishwashers in large kitchens or butler's pantries — not as the primary unit.
