# Kitchen Design Rules & Constraints for AI Kitchen Designer Training

A comprehensive rule set compiled from NKBA guidelines, building codes, and professional designer best practices. Rules are organized by category, with hard constraints (code/safety) distinguished from strong recommendations (best practice).

---

## 1. THE WORK TRIANGLE & WORKFLOW

### Hard Rules
- Each leg of the work triangle (sink, range, refrigerator) must be between **4 and 9 feet**.
- The sum of all three legs must be between **13 and 26 feet**.
- No major traffic path should cross through the work triangle.
- No island, peninsula, or other obstruction should intersect a leg of the work triangle.

### Strong Recommendations
- The sink should be positioned **between** the refrigerator and the range/cooktop, since it is the most-used station and serves as the bridge between cold storage and cooking.
- The range/cooktop should **never** be directly across from the sink in a galley layout (splash/burn hazard, and opposing doors create collisions).
- In a multi-cook kitchen, consider **zone-based design** (prep zone, cook zone, clean zone) rather than a strict triangle.

---

## 2. RANGE / COOKTOP PLACEMENT

### Hard Rules
- **Never place a range or cooktop directly in a corner.** The adjacent wall blocks access to the rear burner, creates a fire hazard from grease splatter on the wall, and eliminates the required landing area on that side.
- Provide a minimum of **15" of landing area** on one side of a range and **12"** on the other side (NKBA). Some designers insist on 15" on both sides.
- For a cooktop on an island, provide at least **9" of counter behind** the cooktop (at the same height).
- Maintain **24" minimum clearance** between the cooktop and a protected (noncombustible) surface above. **30" minimum** to an unprotected/combustible surface above.
- Range hood must be **30"–36" above a gas cooktop** and **24"–30" above an electric cooktop**.
- Range hood must be ducted to the outside; minimum exhaust rate of **100 CFM** (150 CFM recommended).
- The range hood (or ventilation) should be at least as wide as the cooktop, ideally **3" wider on each side**.

### Strong Recommendations
- Never place a range directly next to a tall cabinet, tall pantry, or refrigerator without at least a **3" filler** or heat shield. Heat damages adjacent cabinetry and the fridge compressor.
- Never place a range next to a window — curtains are a fire hazard, and cross-breezes interfere with gas flames.
- Never place a range where its door, when open, blocks a primary walkway.
- Avoid placing the range on an island in a kitchen with small children if the island is accessible from all sides (no barrier between burners and traffic).
- A range next to a wall oven stack creates an awkward "heat corridor" — leave at least **9"–12"** of counter between them.

---

## 3. REFRIGERATOR PLACEMENT

### Hard Rules
- Provide at least **15" of landing area on the handle side** of the refrigerator (NKBA).
- For a side-by-side fridge, provide **15" of landing on either side**.
- If no adjacent counter exists, provide **15" of landing no more than 48" across** from the fridge front.
- Leave **½" of clearance** on sides and top for ventilation. If against a wall on the hinge side, leave **2½" minimum**.

### Strong Recommendations
- **Never place the refrigerator directly next to the range/cooktop.** The heat forces the compressor to overwork. Maintain at least **9" of counter** between them (many designers say **15"–24"**).
- The refrigerator should be at the **end of a cabinet run**, not buried in the middle, so the door swing doesn't block the workflow.
- The fridge door, when open at 90°, must not block the main kitchen entry or a primary traffic lane.
- When a fridge is in a corner, ensure the door can open a full **170°** so bins and drawers can be pulled out. Account for the wall/cabinet on the hinge side — you typically need a **3"–6" filler panel**.
- The refrigerator should ideally be the **first appliance you reach** when entering the kitchen from the main entry (grocery unloading logic).
- Counter-depth refrigerators are preferred in smaller kitchens to avoid the fridge jutting out past the counter line.

---

## 4. SINK PLACEMENT

### Hard Rules
- Provide at least **24" of landing area** on one side of the sink and **18"** on the other side (NKBA).
- At least **36" of continuous prep counter** (24" deep) adjacent to a sink.
- All sink-area receptacles must have **GFCI protection**.

### Strong Recommendations
- If there is a window on the sink wall, **center the sink under the window.** This is one of the most universally expected design conventions — deviating from it looks like a mistake.
- If centering is impossible (e.g., the window is off-center on the wall), **center the window visually relative to the cabinet run** rather than leaving an obvious asymmetry.
- Never place the sink in a corner — it's ergonomically terrible (you can't stand squarely in front of it) and wastes counter space.
- The sink should have a **direct, unobstructed path to the dishwasher** (ideally immediately adjacent).
- An island sink should face the main kitchen work area, not the living room (dirty dishes in plain view of guests).
- Avoid placing the sink more than **a few steps from the range** — you carry water/pots between them constantly.
- A prep sink on an island is fine, but a **primary cleanup sink on an island** requires careful thought about sightlines to the dining/living area.

## 5. DISHWASHER PLACEMENT

### Hard Rules
- The dishwasher must be **immediately adjacent to the sink** — either left or right side, no more than **36"** from the sink center (plumbing requirement and usability).
- Provide at least **21" of clear standing space** between the dishwasher edge and any perpendicular countertop, appliance, or cabinet.

### Strong Recommendations
- Place the dishwasher on the **side of the sink closest to the primary dish cabinet/drawer** storage (so the unload path is short).
- For a right-handed user, the dishwasher is typically best on the **right** side of the sink (rinse with right hand, load with left).
- The dishwasher door, when open, must not block the sink, the range, or a primary walkway.
- Never place a dishwasher at a right angle to the sink in a corner — you can't have both open at the same time.
- In a two-dishwasher setup, place them on **opposite sides of the sink**.
- In an island, a dishwasher should face the interior of the kitchen, not a seating area (steam/noise directed away from guests).

---

## 6. ISLAND & PENINSULA RULES

### Hard Rules
- Minimum clearance around an island: **42"** for a single-cook kitchen, **48"** for a multi-cook kitchen (NKBA).
- If your kitchen is less than **13 feet wide**, an island is generally not recommended.

### Strong Recommendations
- An island should be at minimum **4 feet long × 2 feet deep** to be functional. Below that, it's just an obstacle.
- Islands with both a sink and cooktop need to be at least **9–10 feet long**.
- Never make an island so large that it pushes the clearance to the perimeter counters below the 42" minimum.
- The island should be **parallel** to the longest run of cabinets — a skewed or rotated island looks like a mistake unless the room's architecture justifies it.
- Avoid putting so many appliances in the island that there's no usable counter left on it.
- Islands should have **at least one electrical outlet** (pop-up style preferred per current code updates — side-mounted outlets on islands are discouraged).
- If the island has seating on one side and a cooktop on the other, provide at least **12"–15"** of counter between the cooktop and the seating overhang to protect seated guests from splatter.

## 7. SEATING & COUNTER HEIGHT

### Hard Rules
- Allow **24" of counter width per seat** for standard stools.
- Allow **30"–32" center-to-center** for stools with armrests.
- Leave at least **6"** between the outermost stool and the edge of the counter.

### Strong Recommendations
- Counter overhang for seating: **12"–15"** (10" absolute minimum for knee clearance).
- **10"–12"** between the seat and the underside of the counter for legroom.
- At least **36"** of clearance behind seated guests in a low-traffic area; **44"–48"** if it's a primary walkway.
- Stool heights: **24"–26"** for a 36" counter; **28"–30"** for a 42" bar-height counter.
- Never combine seating and a sink on the same side of an island — guests sitting at the counter don't want to face dirty dishes and splashing water.

---

## 8. COUNTERTOP & LANDING AREAS

### Hard Rules
- A total of **158" of countertop frontage** (24" deep, 15" clearance above) is the NKBA recommended minimum for a functional kitchen.
- Prep area: at least **36" wide × 24" deep** of continuous countertop.

### Strong Recommendations
- **Never have zero landing area next to an appliance.** Every appliance (oven, microwave, fridge, range) needs adjacent counter for setting things down.
- The longest unbroken stretch of counter should be in the **primary prep zone** (between sink and range).
- Avoid putting a seam in a countertop at a high-use area (near the sink or prep zone) — seams attract moisture and bacteria.
- A countertop that terminates at a doorway or traffic lane should have a **rounded or eased edge** to prevent hip injuries.
- Waterfall edges on islands look sleek, but ensure the material budget accounts for the extra slab area.

---

## 9. CABINET DESIGN RULES

### Hard Rules
- Upper cabinets must be at least **18"** above the countertop (standard).
- Blind corner cabinets require a **3"–5" pull** from the wall and a **3"+ filler strip** between the blind cabinet and the adjacent cabinet.
- End panels / finished end skins must be applied to any exposed cabinet side (facing a doorway, open to a room, etc.).

### Strong Recommendations
- **Never leave a dead corner.** Use a lazy Susan, blind corner cabinet with pull-out, or a corner drawer system. Empty corner base cabinets are wasted space.
- Cabinet doors in a corner must not collide when opened simultaneously — verify swing arcs.
- Drawers are almost always preferable to doors on base cabinets (accessibility, visibility, and storage efficiency).
- The top shelf of wall cabinets should be no higher than **72"** (reachable without a step stool for most users); if cabinets go to the ceiling, the top section should store rarely-used items.
- Never place a wall cabinet so that it blocks a window or cuts into a window's trim.
- Pull-out trash/recycling should be located near the **main prep area and/or sink** — not across the kitchen.
- Minimum trash cabinet width: **15"** for a single bin, **18"** for a dual bin (trash + recycling).
- Spice storage or a narrow pull-out should be **within arm's reach of the range**, not on the other side of the kitchen.

## 10. WALKWAYS & CLEARANCES

### Hard Rules
- Work aisle (between opposing counters/appliances): **42" minimum** for one cook, **48" minimum** for two cooks (NKBA).
- Walkway (not a work aisle, just passing through): **36" minimum**.
- A doorway into the kitchen should be at least **32" clear** (34" recommended).

### Strong Recommendations
- **No appliance door, when open, should block a doorway or reduce a walkway below 36".**
- Oven doors, dishwasher doors, and refrigerator doors open into the walkway — plan for all of them to be open simultaneously in the worst case.
- Two opposing appliance doors (e.g., oven across from dishwasher) should never be able to collide when both are open.
- A kitchen entry door should not swing into the kitchen and hit a cabinet or appliance.
- In a galley kitchen, if both ends are open (walk-through), expect traffic and do **not** place the range where passing traffic is behind the cook.

## 11. ELECTRICAL & LIGHTING

### Hard Rules
- **GFCI protection** on all kitchen countertop receptacles (NEC requirement).
- No point along a countertop wall should be more than **24"** from a receptacle (translates to roughly every **48"**).
- At least **two 20-amp small-appliance circuits** serving countertop receptacles.
- A dedicated **20-amp circuit** for the dishwasher.
- A dedicated circuit for the refrigerator (recommended 20-amp).
- A dedicated circuit for the range/oven (40- or 50-amp for electric).

### Strong Recommendations
- Under-cabinet lighting is nearly mandatory in a well-designed kitchen — countertops in shadow are a usability and safety issue.
- Pendant lights over an island: the bottom of the pendant should hang **30"–36"** above the island surface. Use **2 pendants** for a 6-foot island, **3** for 8+ feet.
- Recessed lights: general rule is to space them **equal to half the ceiling height** apart (e.g., 4' apart for 8' ceilings).
- Always put island/pendant lights and under-cabinet lights on **separate switches** from the main ceiling lights. Dimmers on everything.
- A light directly over the sink (recessed can or pendant) is critical.
- Avoid placing a recessed light directly above where someone stands (creates shadows on the counter in front of them). Offset toward the counter edge.
- Outlet placement on islands: **pop-up or flush-mount** outlets are strongly preferred. Outlets on the side of an island at child height are a hazard and are increasingly discouraged by code.

## 12. DOOR & WINDOW INTERACTIONS

### Strong Recommendations
- **Never place a range under a window.** Curtains are a fire hazard, and the window can't be easily opened/closed when the range is hot.
- **Center the sink under a window if one exists on the sink wall.** This is arguably the single most expected convention in kitchen design.
- Upper cabinets should **never obscure or partially overlap a window.** If the cabinet run conflicts with a window, use open shelving or stop the cabinets short with a finished end panel.
- A range hood/vent should not be ducted through a window. This sounds obvious, but verify duct routing early in design.
- If a kitchen door has a window/glass panel, don't place the refrigerator directly behind it (someone opening the door from outside can't see the person at the fridge and may hit them).

## 13. AESTHETIC & PROPORTION RULES

### Strong Recommendations
- **Symmetry matters around focal points.** If the range has a decorative hood, the cabinets flanking it should be the same width. The same applies to a window over the sink.
- Cabinet filler strips should be on the **least visible side** (typically against a wall or in a corner, not flanking a focal point).
- If uppers and lowers are different widths, at least align the **major vertical lines** (e.g., the edge of the sink base aligns with the edge of the upper cabinet above it).
- Toe kick height should be consistent: **3.5"–4"** standard. Inconsistent toe kicks look like a mistake.
- If the kitchen has a mix of cabinet heights (e.g., 36" base, 42" uppers, a 96" pantry), create a **visual hierarchy** — don't scatter heights randomly.
- Crown molding or a flat trim at the ceiling: pick one approach and carry it across the entire kitchen. Don't mix.
- Hardware placement should be consistent: pulls horizontal on drawers, vertical on doors (or all horizontal, but be consistent).
- Open shelving looks great when styled but becomes visual clutter in real use — use sparingly, ideally flanking a window or range hood for symmetry.
- Avoid mixing more than **two countertop materials** in a kitchen (e.g., one for perimeter, one for island). Three materials looks chaotic.
- Island material/color should **contrast** with the perimeter — a matching island in a small kitchen makes the room feel like one undifferentiated box.

## 14. SAFETY RULES

### Hard Rules
- No open shelving above a cooktop/range — wall cabinets or a range hood only.
- Range anti-tip bracket must be installed (code requirement, prevents the range from tipping if someone stands on the open oven door).
- GFCIs on all countertop receptacles (see electrical section above).
- Tempered glass required for any window within **24"** of a door.

### Strong Recommendations
- **Never place a range at the end of a cabinet run where a traffic path passes behind the cook.** Pot handles sticking out over the traffic lane are a burn hazard, especially with children.
- Knobs on the **front** of a range are more accessible and prevent reaching over hot burners. But if small children are present, consider models with rear-mounted or lockable controls.
- Never run a vent duct with a reduction in size from the vent hood to the exterior termination — it reduces airflow and creates a grease trap.
- Avoid placing a microwave where children can reach it but can't safely remove hot items (i.e., don't mount it too high for the shortest adult user — **above 54"** from the floor is too high for safe use by most people).
- Non-slip flooring in front of the sink and range.

---

## 15. PLUMBING & UTILITY RULES

### Hard Rules
- The sink must have a **properly vented drain** (usually via a vent stack or air-admittance valve).
- Dishwasher drain must include a **high loop or air gap** to prevent backflow.
- Gas lines must have an **accessible shutoff valve** within the room.

### Strong Recommendations
- Plan plumbing early — moving a sink more than a few feet from existing plumbing is expensive (especially in slab-on-grade construction).
- An island sink requires either **routing drain/vent through the floor** or an air-admittance valve (check local code).
- A pot-filler faucet over the range should have its own shutoff valve accessible without pulling the range out.
- Don't forget a water line for the refrigerator (ice maker/water dispenser) — run it during rough-in, not as an afterthought.

## 16. COMMON "A DESIGNER WOULD NEVER" SUMMARY

1. **Never** put a range/cooktop in a corner.
2. **Never** put a range directly next to a refrigerator.
3. **Never** put a range under a window.
4. **Never** put a sink in a corner.
5. **Never** leave a dead, inaccessible corner cabinet.
6. **Never** have zero counter/landing space next to any appliance.
7. **Never** allow two opposing appliance doors to collide when open.
8. **Never** place the dishwasher far from the sink.
9. **Never** block a window with a wall cabinet.
10. **Never** have a walkway narrower than 36" (or a work aisle narrower than 42").
11. **Never** skip the range hood/ventilation.
12. **Never** run a traffic path behind the cook at the range.
13. **Never** let the main kitchen entry door hit an appliance or cabinet when opened.
14. **Never** off-center a sink when a window is directly on the sink wall.
15. **Never** place a fridge where the door can't open past 90° due to a wall.
16. **Never** design an island that pushes clearance below 42" anywhere.
17. **Never** place seating directly adjacent to a cooktop with no buffer counter.
18. **Never** forget landing space for the microwave — it needs 15" adjacent.
19. **Never** mix more than two countertop materials.
20. **Never** put the trash/recycling pull-out far from the prep/sink zone.
21. **Never** place upper cabinets at varying heights unless architecturally intentional.
22. **Never** install a range without an anti-tip bracket.
23. **Never** position recessed lights directly above the cook's head (shadows on the counter).
24. **Never** design an island with seating that faces directly into a dirty-dish sink.
25. **Never** skip GFCI protection on countertop outlets.
