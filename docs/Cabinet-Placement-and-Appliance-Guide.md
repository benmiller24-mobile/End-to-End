# Cabinet Placement & Appliance Selection Guide

*A design reference for the Kitchen Design Engine — where each cabinet belongs, which appliances suit each layout, and brand recommendations from value to ultra-premium. Grounded in the NKBA Kitchen Planning Guidelines and current professional practice.*

---

## How to read this guide

The engine builds five base layouts: **single-wall, galley, L-shape, U-shape,** and **island/peninsula**. Two questions decide the quality of any of them:

1. **Does each cabinet do the right job in the right place?** That is governed by the *work zones* and a short list of cabinet-type rules.
2. **Are the appliances right for the layout?** A 48" range is a gift in a long single-wall kitchen and a mistake in a tight galley. This guide maps appliances — and specific brands at three budget tiers — to each layout.

---

# Part 1 — Where each cabinet goes

## 1.1 The five work zones

Modern kitchen planning has largely replaced the lone "work triangle" with **five task zones**. You group storage and surfaces by the task they serve, so a cook never has to cross another cook's space. Sketch the zones before choosing a single cabinet.

| Zone | What lives here | Where it goes |
|---|---|---|
| **Consumables** | Refrigerator, pantry, dry goods | Nearest the entry, so groceries don't travel across the room |
| **Non-consumables** | Everyday dishes, glassware, serving pieces | Immediately beside the dishwasher — unloading should be a pivot, not a walk |
| **Cleaning / waste** | Sink, dishwasher, trash + recycling pull-out | The operational center; dishwasher within **36"** of the sink, **21"** standing clearance at its open door |
| **Preparation** | The largest run of uninterrupted counter, knife/utensil drawers, prep sink | Between the cleaning and cooking zones — this is the most-used surface in the kitchen |
| **Cooking** | Range *or* cooktop + wall oven, ventilation, pots/pans drawers, spice + oil | Anchored by the heat source; landing counter on both sides |

The single most valuable arrangement rule: **store each item where it is first used, sized to the thing being stored, in a single layer** so nothing hides behind anything else.

## 1.2 Cabinet-type rules (which cabinet, which spot)

These are the rules a high-end designer applies almost reflexively. They are the ones worth encoding because they convert "a box of the right width" into "the right cabinet."

- **Drawers beat doors for base cabinets.** Use **drawer bases (B3D/B4D)** for pots, pans, dishes, and utensils wherever possible. Reserve **door bases** for the under-sink cabinet and for tall/awkward items that don't suit drawers.
- **Flank the range** with a narrow base on each side — a **9"–12" pull-out** (spice/tray/utensil) gives the cook landing storage within reach and prevents the range from butting against a tall cabinet or wall.
- **Sink base = door base** (plumbing lives below), with the **trash/recycling pull-out immediately adjacent** so waste handling is at the prep point. The engine already auto-adds a double-bin waste unit (BWDMA) near the sink.
- **Under a cooktop, use a deep drawer base** for pots and bakeware (you don't need an oven cavity there). Under a standing range, that storage comes with the appliance.
- **Tray/​sheet-pan dividers** belong in a narrow vertical cabinet **next to the oven** — baking sheets, boards, and cooling racks stored upright where they're pulled for baking.
- **Tall cabinets (pantry, oven tower, fridge surround) group together at the end of a run**, never marooned mid-run where they break the counter and cast the work surface into a corner. The engine enforces tall-unit grouping.
- **Corners get a functional device, not dead space:** a lazy/super-susan (BL-SS) on a corner that turns, or a blind-corner pull-out (BBC-MC magic corner) where one run runs past the other. The engine auto-substitutes these.
- **Fillers** absorb the fractional inches: ≥1.5" at a wall, ≥2" at an inside corner so two perpendicular doors/drawers clear each other.

## 1.3 Placement by layout

**Single-wall.** A triangle is geometrically impossible, so lean entirely on the zone sequence along the wall: **fridge → prep/landing → sink (+ dishwasher + waste) → prep → cooking**, with tall units bookending the ends. Put the fridge nearest the entry. If an island is present, move the *prep* and a *seating/casual* zone to it.

**Galley.** Two facing runs naturally form the triangle. The classic split is **sink + refrigerator on one wall, range opposite** — keep the cook from pivoting into traffic. Maintain ≥42" (48" for two cooks) between the runs.

**L-shape.** Put the **refrigerator at one leg-end, the range at the other, and the sink in the middle** of the run — often under a window. The corner takes a susan or blind-corner unit. Tall units cluster at a leg-end so the two legs stay open work surface.

**U-shape.** **Refrigerator + sink on one leg, range on the opposite leg**, giving a tight, efficient triangle inside the U. Each leg-end can carry tall storage; the back-of-U wall is prime uninterrupted prep counter.

**Island / peninsula.** The island absorbs whatever the perimeter can't hold gracefully — most often the **prep zone** (a second prep sink + trash), **seating**, and sometimes the **cooking zone** (cooktop with downdraft or a ceiling hood). Keep cooking and water at least one zone apart on the island, and respect the clearances in Part 2.6.

# Part 2 — Appliances by layout

## 2.1 The work triangle, briefly

The three triangle points — **sink, cooking surface, refrigerator** — should sit **4–9 ft apart per leg**, **13–26 ft total**, with no leg crossed by a cabinet run by more than ~12". It's a sanity check, not a straitjacket; the zone logic above does the heavier lifting in modern kitchens.

## 2.2 Layout → appliance fit

| Layout | Natural cooking choice | Refrigeration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Single-wall** | Slide-in **range** (saves wall length) — or a cooktop with the oven in an island | Counter-depth or column near the entry end | Length is the constraint; a 48" range only if the wall is long (≥10–11 ft of cabinetry) |
| **Galley** | **Range** on one wall (compact) | Fridge with the sink on the opposite wall | Avoid a 48" range unless aisles stay ≥42"; ventilation must clear the facing run |
| **L-shape** | Range *or* cooktop+wall-oven; oven tower at a leg-end | Column or French-door at a leg-end | Most flexible layout; supports a full pro suite |
| **U-shape** | Cooktop + **wall oven** shines (oven in a tall leg-end) | Column refrigeration integrated into a leg | Three walls give room to split cooking into cooktop + dedicated ovens |
| **Island** | **Cooktop in the island** (downdraft or island hood) or keep cooking on the wall | Perimeter; island gets a **prep sink** + beverage/wine | Don't put the primary range in a small island; ventilation gets expensive |

## 2.3 Range vs. cooktop + wall oven

**Choose a range when** space is tight (a 30" range replaces a 30" cooktop *plus* a separate oven cabinet), the budget is finite, or you want the cooking suite as a focal point. Simplest for everyday cooking.

**Choose a cooktop + wall oven when** you cook for a crowd or bake often (two ovens, more simultaneous capacity), want the oven at a back-friendly height, or want a seamless built-in look with the cooktop disappearing into the counter. This split suits **L** and **U** layouts that have a tall run to host the oven(s).

## 2.4 Ventilation sizing (don't skip this)

- **Hood width** = cooktop width, ideally **+6"** of overhang (a 48" range wants a 54"–60" hood).
- **CFM** rule of thumb: cooktop width in inches × **100 (electric/induction)** or × **150 (gas)**. A 48" gas range lands roughly **600–1,200 CFM**; a 48" range generally needs **300–600+ CFM** minimum.
- **Mounting height:** gas **24"–30"** above the surface; never more than **36"** cooktop-to-hood for capture.
- High-CFM hoods (≥400 CFM) usually require **make-up air** by code — flag it on the plan.

## 2.5 Fuel: induction vs. gas vs. dual-fuel

- **Induction** — fastest, most precise, coolest and safest surface, easiest to clean; pairs beautifully with a cooktop+wall-oven build. Best default for modern/integrated kitchens.
- **Gas / dual-fuel** — traditional feel, instant visual feedback, high-BTU searing (pro ranges hit 18,000–25,000 BTU); dual-fuel adds an electric oven for more even baking.

## 2.6 Island appliance clearances (recap of the engine's rules)

- **42"** work aisle to opposing runs (48" for two cooks); **36"** absolute minimum walkway.
- **48"** on a **seating** side (stools + a pass-behind); **36"** behind a seat with no through-traffic, **44"** if a walkway passes behind.
- A **cooktop in an island** wants 42" clear on **all** sides (fire/landing) and either downdraft or a ceiling-mounted hood.
- Seat knee depth by counter height: **18"** at 30", **15"** at 36", **12"** at 42"; **24"** of width per seat.

# Part 3 — Appliance recommendations (value → ultra) by layout

Three tiers, so the recommendation scales to budget. The **Luxury** and **Ultra** columns use the brands already in the engine's catalog (Sub-Zero, Wolf, Cove, Thermador, Miele, Gaggenau, BlueStar); the **Value** column names a sensible step-down.

## 3.1 Brand strengths (what each does best)

- **Sub-Zero** — the benchmark in **refrigeration** (dual compressors, air purification); columns integrate seamlessly.
- **Wolf** — professional **ranges, rangetops, and ovens**; the natural cooking partner to Sub-Zero.
- **Cove** — Sub-Zero/Wolf's **dishwasher**, built to match the suite.
- **Thermador** — strong all-rounder and the **value leader** among the luxury names; **Star burner**, Sapphire dishwasher, generous package rebates.
- **Miele** — best-in-class **dishwashers** and **steam/​combi-steam ovens**; quiet, long-lived; lower entry price than Sub-Zero on refrigeration columns.
- **Gaggenau** — design-forward **wall ovens and steam ovens**; the architectural showpiece ("the Maserati of appliances").
- **BlueStar** — pro **ranges** with very high BTU output and 1,000+ custom colors/finishes; also panel-ready columns — character a stainless suite can't match.
- **Bosch** (value) — the reliability leader in **dishwashers**; clean built-in look. **KitchenAid / Café / JennAir** (value-to-mid) — credible ranges, wall ovens, and panel-ready refrigeration at a fraction of pro pricing.

## 3.2 Category recommendations

| Category | Value | Luxury | Ultra-premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Range (gas/dual-fuel)** | KitchenAid / Café 30"–36" | **Wolf** dual-fuel 36"–48", **Thermador** Pro Grand | **BlueStar** Platinum, **La Cornue** |
| **Cooktop (induction)** | Bosch / KitchenAid induction | **Thermador** Freedom induction, **Miele** | **Gaggenau** Vario / full-surface |
| **Wall oven(s)** | KitchenAid / Café | **Wolf** M-series, **Miele** | **Gaggenau** 400-series, **Miele** combi-steam |
| **Refrigeration** | Café / JennAir panel-ready | **Sub-Zero** built-in, **Thermador** Freedom columns | **Sub-Zero** PRO 48, **Gaggenau** Vario columns |
| **Dishwasher** | **Bosch** 300/500/800 | **Miele**, **Thermador** Sapphire | **Cove**, **Miele** G7000 |
| **Ventilation** | Broan / Zephyr | **Wolf**, **Thermador** | **Gaggenau**, custom insert + chimney |

## 3.3 Recommended package by layout

**Single-wall** — keep it compact and clean.
- *Value:* KitchenAid 30" range · Bosch 300 dishwasher · Café counter-depth fridge.
- *Luxury:* Wolf 36" dual-fuel range · Cove dishwasher · Sub-Zero counter-depth or 36" column.
- *Ultra:* Wolf 48" range (if the wall is long) · Cove · Sub-Zero PRO column — anchored by a 54"–60" hood.

**Galley** — efficiency over spectacle.
- *Value:* KitchenAid 30" range opposite a Bosch dishwasher + counter-depth fridge.
- *Luxury:* Thermador 30"–36" range + Sapphire dishwasher · Thermador Freedom fridge column on the opposite wall.
- *Ultra:* Wolf 36" range · Cove dishwasher · Sub-Zero column — keep the hood low-profile so it clears the facing run.

**L-shape** — the flexible workhorse; supports a full split suite.
- *Value:* Café 36" range · Bosch dishwasher · JennAir French-door.
- *Luxury:* Wolf 36" rangetop + M-series wall oven at the leg-end · Cove dishwasher · Sub-Zero column.
- *Ultra:* Gaggenau induction cooktop + 400-series ovens · Miele combi-steam · Sub-Zero PRO columns.

**U-shape** — let the cooktop + wall-oven split shine.
- *Value:* KitchenAid cooktop + single wall oven · Bosch dishwasher · Café columns.
- *Luxury:* Thermador induction cooktop + double wall ovens · Miele dishwasher · Thermador Freedom columns.
- *Ultra:* Gaggenau Vario cooktop + 400-series double ovens + steam · Cove dishwasher · Sub-Zero columns split across two legs.

**Island / peninsula** — perimeter does the heavy cooking; the island adds prep, seating, and a beverage zone.
- *Value:* perimeter range + Bosch dishwasher; island gets a **prep sink** + KitchenAid beverage center.
- *Luxury:* perimeter Wolf range + Cove dishwasher; island **prep sink + Sub-Zero undercounter wine/beverage**; seating with a 15" overhang.
- *Ultra:* island **Gaggenau or BlueStar induction cooktop** with downdraft or a statement ceiling hood + **prep sink**; perimeter Sub-Zero/Wolf suite; Cove dishwasher near the cleanup sink.
